# US-004 — Offer Letter Generation, Candidate Response Tracking, and Auto-Expiry

## User Story

**As a** candidate,  
**I want** to receive a personalised offer letter with all approved terms, accept or decline it within a defined deadline, and have the offer automatically expire if not responded to,  
**So that** the hiring process concludes with a clear, documented outcome for both parties.

## Business Value

Digital offer management eliminates email attachment confusion and provides an auditable acceptance record. Automatic expiry enforces hiring velocity SLAs and frees up the position for re-opening if the candidate does not respond.

## Acceptance Criteria

### Scenario 1: Offer letter generated with token resolution
**Given** the approval chain is complete  
**When** offer generation is triggered  
**Then** all template tokens (`{{candidate_name}}`, `{{role_title}}`, `{{start_date}}`, `{{salary}}`) are resolved, a PDF is generated, stored in Supabase Storage, and the candidate receives an email with a "View Offer" link within 10 minutes.

### Scenario 2: Candidate accepts offer
**Given** the candidate opens the offer portal and clicks "Accept"  
**When** acceptance is recorded  
**Then** the application status changes to `"offer_accepted"`, an acceptance confirmation email is sent to the candidate and recruiter, and an onboarding task is queued.

### Scenario 3: Candidate declines offer
**Given** the candidate clicks "Decline" and provides an optional reason  
**When** the decline is recorded  
**Then** the application status changes to `"offer_declined"`, the recruiter is notified, and the requisition vacancy count is updated.

### Scenario 4: Offer auto-expires after deadline
**Given** the offer deadline (default 5 business days) passes without a response  
**When** the BullMQ deadline job fires  
**Then** the offer status changes to `"expired"`, the candidate receives an expiry notification, and the recruiter is notified to decide on next steps.
