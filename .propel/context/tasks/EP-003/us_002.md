# US-002 — AI Resume Parsing Worker with BullMQ and spaCy NER

## User Story

**As a** recruiter,  
**I want** candidate resumes automatically parsed by an AI worker that extracts structured data (name, email, skills, years of experience, education, employment history) within 30 seconds of upload,  
**So that** HR reviewers see pre-populated candidate data without manual data entry.

## Business Value

Automated parsing eliminates manual data extraction from resumes, reducing HR review time per candidate from minutes to seconds. Structured output feeds the AI screening engine directly.

## Acceptance Criteria

### Scenario 1: Parsing completes within 30 s of queue entry
**Given** a clean resume file is enqueued in BullMQ  
**When** the Python/spaCy worker picks up the job  
**Then** the parsed result is written to the `resumes.parsed_data` JSONB column within 30 seconds.

### Scenario 2: Extracted fields match resume content
**Given** a resume contains "5 years at Acme Corp as Senior Backend Engineer" and lists "Python, Node.js, PostgreSQL" skills  
**When** parsing completes  
**Then** `parsed_data.experience_years ≥ 5`, `parsed_data.skills` includes "Python", "Node.js", "PostgreSQL", and `parsed_data.employers[0].name = "Acme Corp"`.

### Scenario 3: Parsing failure triggers retry with backoff
**Given** the spaCy worker throws an exception on a malformed PDF  
**When** BullMQ handles the failure  
**Then** the job is retried up to 3 times with exponential backoff; after 3 failures the job moves to the dead-letter queue and the resume status is set to `"parse_failed"`.

### Scenario 4: Worker scales to handle burst of 50 concurrent jobs
**Given** 50 resumes are uploaded simultaneously  
**When** all jobs are enqueued  
**Then** all 50 are processed within 3 minutes and no jobs are dropped.
