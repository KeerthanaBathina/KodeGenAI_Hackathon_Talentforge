# US-002 — Multi-Step Application Form with Auto-Save and Draft Persistence

## User Story

**As a** candidate,  
**I want** to complete a 4-step application form that auto-saves my progress every 60 seconds and lets me resume from where I left off,  
**So that** I never lose my work if I close the browser or get interrupted mid-application.

## Business Value

Auto-save with draft persistence directly reduces application abandonment. Industry data shows multi-step forms with no save capability lose 40–60% of starts. A resumable draft increases completed application rates.

## Acceptance Criteria

### Scenario 1: Auto-save writes draft after 60 s of inactivity
**Given** a candidate is on Step 2 of the application form  
**When** 60 seconds pass without a form change  
**Then** a "Draft saved" toast appears and the draft is persisted to the database.

### Scenario 2: Candidate resumes incomplete application
**Given** a candidate closed the browser on Step 3  
**When** they return to the requisition listing and click "Continue Application"  
**Then** they are taken to Step 3 with all previously entered data pre-populated.

### Scenario 3: Step navigation validates current step before advancing
**Given** the candidate is on Step 1 with a required field empty  
**When** they click "Next"  
**Then** inline validation errors are shown on the empty fields and navigation is blocked.

### Scenario 4: Final submission locks the draft
**Given** the candidate is on Step 4 and all fields are valid  
**When** they click "Submit Application"  
**Then** the application status changes from `"draft"` to `"submitted"`, the auto-save stops, and the form becomes read-only.
