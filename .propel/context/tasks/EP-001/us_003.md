# US-003 — Login with Role-Based Routing, Account Lockout, and SSO

## User Story

**As a** platform user,  
**I want** to log in with my email/password or via Google/GitHub OAuth and be routed automatically to the dashboard appropriate for my role,  
**So that** I access only the features my role permits and my account is protected against brute-force attacks.

## Business Value

Role-based post-login routing prevents candidates from seeing HR views and vice versa, enforcing separation of concerns without per-route permission checks. Account lockout stops credential-stuffing attacks before they succeed.

## Acceptance Criteria

### Scenario 1: Successful login routes to correct dashboard
**Given** a user with role `hr` logs in  
**When** authentication succeeds  
**Then** they are redirected to `/hr/dashboard`; a `candidate` is redirected to `/candidate/applications`; `recruiter` to `/recruiter/requisitions`; `admin` to `/admin/dashboard`.

### Scenario 2: Account locked after 5 failed attempts
**Given** a candidate enters an incorrect password  
**When** they fail 5 consecutive times  
**Then** the account is locked for 30 minutes, subsequent attempts return HTTP 423 with a "Account temporarily locked" message, and an email notification is sent to the account owner.

### Scenario 3: Google SSO creates or links account
**Given** a candidate clicks "Continue with Google"  
**When** they authenticate with Google  
**Then** if the email is new, an account is auto-created with `status = "active"` and the candidate is sent to onboarding; if the email already exists, the account is linked and they proceed to their dashboard.

### Scenario 4: JWT issued with correct claims
**Given** a successful login  
**When** the JWT is decoded  
**Then** it contains `sub` (userId), `role`, `candidateId` (if applicable), `iat`, and `exp` (24-hour expiry); it is signed with RS256 and stored in an `httpOnly` cookie.
