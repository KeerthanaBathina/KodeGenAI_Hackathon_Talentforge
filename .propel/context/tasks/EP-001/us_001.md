# US-001 — Candidate Self-Registration with OTP Email Verification

## User Story

**As a** job candidate,  
**I want** to create an account using my email address and verify it with a one-time passcode,  
**So that** I have a secure, uniquely identified account that unlocks access to the application portal.

## Business Value

Verified email identity prevents duplicate accounts and ensures all platform communications reach the candidate. A unique candidate ID enables consistent tracking throughout the hiring lifecycle.

## Acceptance Criteria

### Scenario 1: Successful registration and OTP delivery
**Given** a candidate provides a valid email and password on the registration form  
**When** they submit  
**Then** their account is created with `status = "pending_verification"`, a 6-digit OTP is sent to their email within 30 seconds, and they are redirected to the OTP entry screen.

### Scenario 2: Correct OTP activates the account
**Given** a candidate enters the 6-digit OTP from their email  
**When** they submit within 15 minutes of issue  
**Then** their account status changes to `"active"`, a unique `candidate_id` is assigned, and they are redirected to the onboarding profile page.

### Scenario 3: Expired OTP shows error and offers resend
**Given** a candidate enters an OTP after it has expired (> 15 minutes)  
**When** they submit  
**Then** an error message "Code expired — please request a new one" is displayed and a resend link is offered.

### Scenario 4: Duplicate email registration rejected
**Given** an email already exists in the system  
**When** a new registration is attempted with the same email  
**Then** the response does not reveal whether the account exists (generic message: "If this email is new to us, you will receive a verification code"), preventing user enumeration.
