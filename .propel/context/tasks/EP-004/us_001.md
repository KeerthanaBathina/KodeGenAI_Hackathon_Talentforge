# US-001 — HR Review Queue with Column Filters and SLA Countdown Timers

## User Story

**As an** HR reviewer,  
**I want** a sortable, filterable review queue showing all applications awaiting decision, with SLA countdown timers that turn amber at 50% and red at 80% of the SLA window elapsed,  
**So that** I can prioritise applications nearing their deadline and avoid SLA breaches.

## Business Value

SLA visibility ensures no candidate waits beyond agreed response times. Visual countdown timers replace manual deadline tracking and reduce the risk of SLA penalties.

## Acceptance Criteria

### Scenario 1: Queue loads with SLA timers for each application
**Given** the HR reviewer opens the review queue  
**When** the page loads  
**Then** each row shows: candidate name, role, AI score, SLA timer (formatted as HH:MM:SS), and decision controls; the list is sorted by SLA ascending by default.

### Scenario 2: SLA timer turns amber at 50% elapsed
**Given** an application has a 48-hour SLA and 24 hours have passed  
**When** the queue re-renders (updates every 60 s)  
**Then** the SLA timer chip changes to amber and the row is moved up in the default sort.

### Scenario 3: SLA timer turns red at 80% elapsed
**Given** an application has a 48-hour SLA and 38.4 hours have passed  
**When** the queue renders  
**Then** the SLA timer chip is red, a "Urgent" badge appears on the row, and a real-time WebSocket notification fires to all HR reviewers.

### Scenario 4: Column filters narrow the queue
**Given** the HR reviewer selects the "Engineering" department filter and "High Score" (> 75) AI score filter  
**When** the filter is applied  
**Then** only Engineering applications with AI score > 75 are shown and the active filter count badge on the filter button updates.
